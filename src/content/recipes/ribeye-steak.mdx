## 1. Preparation Phase (Delta-T)
Salt the steak generously on all surfaces. Place on a wire rack set in a rimmed baking sheet. Refrigerate uncovered for 24 hours to draw out moisture and dry the surface.

<br/>

## 2. Primary Thermal Processing (Oven)
Preheat oven to 250°F (121°C). Insert a leave-in probe thermometer into the thickest part of the steak. Place in oven and roast until internal temperature reaches 115°F (46°C). This process takes approximately 45 minutes to 1 hour.

<Timer client:load seconds={2700} label="Oven Roasting Phase" />

<br/>

## 3. High-Kinetic Sear Phase (Cast Iron)
Remove steak from oven. Preheat a cast iron skillet over maximum heat until smoking. Add 1 tbsp high-smoke-point oil. Sear steak on each flat side for exactly 60 seconds to achieve the Maillard reaction without overcooking the interior layer.

<Timer client:load seconds={60} label="Cast Iron Sear - Side A" />

<Timer client:load seconds={60} label="Cast Iron Sear - Side B" />

<br/>
Rest for 10 minutes before slicing to allow pressure equalization.
